Determining Term Life Insurance: MATH 178C¶

This project explores which family characteristics influence the amount of term life insurance purchased. The goal is to identify significant predictors and understand their relationship to insurance decisions using statistical modeling.

1. Data Import & Preprocessing¶

import numpy as np

We examine the Survey of Consumer Finances (SCF), a nationally representative sample that contains extensive information on assets, liabilities, income, and demographic characteristics of those sampled (potential U.S. customers). We study a random sample of 500 households with positive incomes that were interviewed in the 2004 survey. We initially consider the subset of $n = 275$ families that purchased term life insurance.

We want to determine family characteristics that influence the amount of insurance purchased. For term life insurance, the quantity of insurance is measured by the policy FACE, the amount that the company will pay in the event of the death of the named insured (death benefit). Characteristics that will turn out to be important include annual INCOME; the number of years of EDUCATION of the survey respondent, and the number of household members, NUMHH.

See http://research.bus.wisc.edu/RegActuariesLinks for the data.
